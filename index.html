What is Myopia?

Myopia, also known as short-sightedness or near-sightedness, is a common eye condition that causes distant objects to appear blurry. It occurs when the eyeball is too long or the cornea is too curved. Myopia is a progressive condition, which means that it can worsen over time, reducing enjoyment and ability to do everyday activities. The more short-sighted you are, the bigger the risk of eye conditions such as cataracts, glaucoma, and retinal detachment.

We can now implement a treatment plan that can reduce the rate of worsening of myopia in growing eyes.

What Treatments are Available?

Lifestyle

Your child's environment and habits can influence their risk of developing myopia. We will give you advice on how to improve habits to reduce the risks of developing or worsening myopia.

Spectacles

Hi-tech spectacle lenses can be used to reduce the progression of myopia. These lenses are designed to stimulate the periphery of the eye to encourage the eye to stop growing as quickly.

Contact Lenses

Special soft lenses can be worn, giving control and freedom to your child. Orthokeratology (Ortho-K) lenses can be worn overnight to reshape the cornea and focus the eye, giving clear uncorrected vision during the day, and myopia control.

Pharmaceuticals

Low-dose atropine eye drops instilled daily can be an effective and safe long-term option by itself, or used in combination with other treatment options.
